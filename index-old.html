About us

Located in the eastern part of Baku, the Azerbaijan Shooting Center has been promoting sports and a healthy lifestyle among the youth since its opening on March 9, 2015.

The center is distinguished by its functionality.

The center spans over 123,000 square meters, including shooting lines for up to 80 athletes simultaneously, halls for practical exercises, a doping control hall, weapons storage, a VIP lounge, and a café. It accommodates both professional and amateur shooters, offering facilities for competitions at 10, 25, 50, and 300 meters.

The complex features an indoor 10-meter range for 80 athletes, a 300-meter semi-enclosed range for professional competitions with 40 athletes, and multiple trap and skeet shooting ranges equipped with automated target launchers.

  • Providing social protection for athletes and coaches
  • Supporting sports veterans
  • Advanced training for shooting specialists
  • Organizing national and international competitions

Disciplines

Explore our diverse shooting disciplines and find the perfect training path for your skill level and goals.

  • Pistol

    Pistol shooting focuses on precision, control, and concentration. This discipline is ideal for developing accuracy and consistency, whether you are training recreationally or preparing for competitions.

    Learn more about pistol shooting disciplines and training opportunities.

  • Rifle

    Rifle shooting emphasizes stability, breathing technique, and long-range accuracy. It is suitable for shooters who want to master technical skills and improve their overall shooting discipline.

    Learn more about rifle shooting disciplines and long-range training.

  • Trap

    Trap shooting is a dynamic shotgun discipline where targets are launched away from the shooter at varying angles. It helps improve reaction time, coordination, and shooting rhythm.

    Learn more about trap shooting and clay target training.

  • Skeet

    Skeet shooting challenges shooters with fast-moving targets crossing from different directions. This discipline is perfect for developing timing, precision, and smooth shooting techniques.

    Learn more about skeet shooting and moving target practice.
